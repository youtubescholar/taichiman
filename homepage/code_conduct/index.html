Code of Conduct

All participants are expected to adhere to the following Code of Conduct to ensure a safe, respectful, and fair environment for everyone involved in our martial arts program. By participating in our classes, you agree to abide by these guidelines.

Purpose

The purpose of this Code of Conduct is to promote positive behaviors, ensure a safe and respectful environment, and prevent ineffective or socially inappropriate interactions.

Acknowledgment of Human Imperfection

We recognize that perfection is unattainable and that everyone will make mistakes. This Code serves as a guide to help address behavior and social interactions in and out of individual and group activities.

Promoting Positive Behaviors

  • Start and end conversations appropriately: Start and end communication so that the exchanges reinforce respect and prevent over exaggeration.
  • Express gratitude and disagree constructively: Encourages resilience, promoting an attitude of integrity and perseverance.

Ensuring a Safe and Respectful Environment

  • Maintain eye contact and handle physical contact appropriately: Ensures all interactions, especially physical, are conducted with the utmost respect and consent, adhering to safety standards. While maintaining eye contact may not always be practical during participation in each activity, always prioritize clear communication and mutual respect.
  • Share feelings appropriately and manage frustration: Share your feelings and needs clearly so everyone can understand and respect each other.

Preventing Ineffective or Socially Inappropriate Interactions

  • Communicate clearly and use understandable gestures: Purpose is to promote an effective learning environment.
  • Prevent repetitive behaviors unrelated to tasks: Keep sessions focused and efficient, respecting the time and effort of all members.

Regarding Individual and Group Communication

  • Ask supportive questions and maintain conversations: Builds a supportive community, encouraging continuous improvement and empathy.
  • Change topics helpfully, ensure good timing and clear messaging: Facilitates smooth, respectful, and constructive exchanges.
  • Take turns speaking and match communication to the audience: Ensures inclusivity and respect for diverse learning and communication styles.
  • Clarify misunderstandings and encourage effective communication: Minimizes conflicts and enhances learning through clarity and openness.
  • Using languages: Provide support and use clear, simple language or translation services when necessary and feasible to ensure effective communication.

Respect and Courtesy

  • Show respect to instructors, fellow participants, and staff at all times.
  • Use polite and respectful language. Offensive, discriminatory, or inappropriate comments are not tolerated.
  • Listen attentively when others are speaking and avoid interrupting.

Adherence to Class Rules

  • Follow all instructions given by the instructor promptly and accurately.
  • Arrive on time for classes and notify the instructor in advance if you need to miss a class.
  • Participate fully in all activities unless you have a medical reason or have discussed a modification with the instructor.

Safety and Hygiene

  • Wear appropriate clothing and footwear for martial arts practice. Remove any jewelry or accessories that could cause injury.
  • Maintain personal hygiene to ensure a clean and safe environment for everyone. This includes washing hands, wearing clean attire, and using deodorant.
  • Report any injuries, illnesses, or medical conditions to the instructor before participating in the class.
  • Do not engage in sparring or any physical contact without the instructorâ€™s permission and supervision.

Inclusivity and Accessibility

  • Support and encourage fellow participants, regardless of their skill level, background, or physical abilities.
  • Accommodate and respect the needs of participants with disabilities or accomodations.
  • Foster a welcoming environment where everyone feels valued and included.

Ethical Behavior

  • Use martial arts techniques responsibly and ethically. Do not use skills learned in class to harm others outside of the training environment.
  • Maintain honesty and integrity in all interactions, both within and outside of class.
  • Avoid any form of cheating or dishonesty during training and assessments.

Conflict Resolution

  • Address personal disagreements or conflicts with fellow participants privately and respectfully.
  • Seek outside mediation assistance if conflicts cannot be resolved amicably.
  • Report any instances of bullying, harassment, or inappropriate behavior to the instructor immediately.

Consequences of Violations

  • Verbal Warning: For minor infractions (e.g., lapse in observation, minor disruptions), participants may receive a verbal warning from the instructor. This serves as a reminder to adhere to the code of conduct and correct behavior.
  • Written Warning: Repeated or more serious infractions (e.g., repeated interruptions, disrespectful language) will result in a written warning. The written warning will document the infraction and outline the expected behavior moving forward.
  • Temporary Suspension: Participants who do not adhere to the code despite previous warnings may face temporary suspension from classes. The duration of the suspension will be determined based on the severity of the infraction (e.g., ongoing disruptive behavior, unsafe practices).
  • Permanent Removal: Severe or repeated violations (e.g., physical aggression, harassment, repeated safety violations) may result in permanent removal from the program. This action is reserved for the most serious breaches of the code of conduct and is implemented to ensure the safety and well-being of all participants.
  • Appeals Process: Participants have the right to appeal any disciplinary action. Appeals must be submitted in writing to the head instructor within 7 days of the action. The appeals process aims to ensure fairness and provide an opportunity for reconsideration.

Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience any behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it to the instructor or designated staff member. All reports will be handled confidentially and with discretion.

Review Process

  • Initial Consideration: Upon receiving a report, an initial consideration will be conducted within 7 days to consider the situation and determine the next steps.
  • Review: A thorough review will be carried out, including exploring details regarding all relevant parties and witnesses. This process aims to be completed within 2-4 weeks, depending on the complexity of the case.
  • Resolution: After the review, a decision will be made regarding the appropriate actions to take. Both the reporting and the reported parties will be informed of the outcome within 7 days of the review's conclusion.
  • Appeals: Any party involved in the review may appeal the decision. Appeals must be submitted in writing within 7 days of the decision notification. The appeal will be reviewed and a final decision made within 14 days.

Feedback and Improvement

  • Provide constructive feedback to the instructor to help improve the class experience for everyone.
  • Be open to receiving feedback from the instructor and fellow participants.
  • Continuously strive for personal improvement and growth in your martial arts practice.

Privacy and Confidentiality

  • Respect the privacy of fellow participants. Do not share personal information or details about others without their consent.
  • Obtain permission before recording or photographing any part of the class.
  • Ensure that any shared media respects the privacy and wishes of those involved.

Indemnity Clause

  • Participants are responsible for their own actions and must exercise caution and good judgment during all activities.
  • By participating in the program, you agree to indemnify and hold harmless the instructors, staff, and facility from any claims, damages, or liabilities arising from your participation.
  • Ensure that you understand and accept the risks associated with martial arts practice before engaging in any activities.

Legal Alignment

This document has been crafted in compliance with Massachusetts laws, including the Americans with Disabilities Act and anti-discrimination laws, ensuring that it is legally robust and adheres to the highest standards of safety and equality.

Feedback and Revisions

We welcome and encourage feedback from all community members to ensure this Code remains relevant, effective, and reflective of our shared values. Regular reviews will be conducted to adapt and refine our practices.

By adhering to this Code of Conduct, we can maintain a safe, respectful, and supportive environment for all participants. Thank you for your cooperation and commitment to these standards.
